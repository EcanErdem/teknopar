Routine Dental Check-ups:

Regular examinations by a dentist to assess oral health, identify any issues, and provide preventive care.

Teeth Cleaning (Prophylaxis):

Professional cleaning to remove plaque, tartar, and stains from teeth, promoting oral hygiene and preventing gum diseases.

Fillings and Restorations:

Treatment for cavities or damaged teeth, restoring their structure and function using materials like amalgam, composite resin, or porcelain.

Dental Crowns and Bridges:

Crowns are used to cover and protect damaged or weak teeth, while bridges replace missing teeth by anchoring artificial teeth to adjacent natural teeth.

Root Canal Therapy:

Treatment to remove infected or damaged pulp from inside a tooth, relieving pain and preserving the tooth's structure.

Dental Implants:

Surgical placement of artificial tooth roots into the jawbone to support dental prosthetics like crowns, bridges, or dentures.

Orthodontic Treatment:

Correction of misaligned teeth or jaws using braces, aligners, or other orthodontic appliances to improve oral function and aesthetics.

Oral Surgery:

Surgical procedures such as tooth extraction, gum surgery, or jaw realignment to address complex dental issues.

Cosmetic Dentistry:

Aesthetic treatments like teeth whitening, veneers, bonding, or gum contouring to enhance the appearance of the smile.

Periodontal Care:

Diagnosis and treatment of gum diseases like gingivitis and periodontitis, including scaling, root planing, and gum surgeries.

Pediatric Dentistry:

Dental care specialized for children, focusing on preventive measures, education, and treatments tailored to their unique needs.

Denture Services:

Fabrication, adjustment, repair, and maintenance of partial or complete dentures for patients missing multiple teeth.
